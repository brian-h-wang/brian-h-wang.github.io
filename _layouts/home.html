About Me

I began my PhD studies at Cornell in 2016. Previously, I received my B.A. in Computer Science, also from Cornell.

As a PhD student, I've supervised undergraduate and masters' students in the Autonomous Systems Lab, working on projects including autonomous driving algorithms, robotics simulation, and SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) based on 3-D object detection landmarks.

As a teaching assistant for MAE 5180, Autonomous Mobile Robots, I've also instructed Cornell students on fundamental robotics algorithms for estimation (including Kalman and particle filtering), mapping, SLAM, and path planning. I went on to develop course materials, exercises, and animations for the upcoming robotics program offered by eCornell.

During Fall 2019, I interned at Draper Laboratory in Cambridge, MA, where I worked on vision-based control for autonomous quadrotor drones.

When not working I enjoy snowboarding, rock climbing, and going on hikes around Ithaca, NY. I'm also a second degree black belt in Taekwondo, and as a Cornell student I competed in intercollegiate tournaments in the Eastern Collegiate Taekwondo Conference.
